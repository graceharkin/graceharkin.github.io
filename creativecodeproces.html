Problem

Designers who code and developers who design need a space to learn,
share, and collaborate on skills and projects.

Solution

An online community for web designers/developers hosted on a responsive
website that is available on desktop, tablet, and mobile.

Requirements

Brand identity
Advertising (print, digital, & animated)
User interface
Publication (print)

Personas

Jodi

UX designer for a large, enterprise company. Has worked there for 18 years. Attended university for graphic design and self taught UX design. Loves to paint, has a strong background in visual design, but is keen on user experience. Works well with the numerous developers and offering managers on her product team, but wants to learn more about how to design within the constraints of her developers.

Chris

Currently attends university for Computer Science and has been a full-stack developer intern. He prefers front-end development but also has a passion for design. He has taken art classes but all visual design is self taught. He built his own website but wants to spice it up and start branding himself as a designer as well as a developer.

Jo

Two years ago left his job a large tech company as a Team Lead (traditionally a back-end developer) to pursue his dream of creating a start-up. His company is up and running and he needs to attract young developers and designers to hire. He knows little of the design side, but highly respects the designers he worked with for decades.

UI Inspiration

Illustrations are used for creativecode.io’s website and advertising. These illustrations put users into the context of the product, giving it a narrative look and feel in order to emphasize creativecode.io’s approachability.

creativecode.io’s advertising emphasizes the platform’s accessibility on desktop, tablet, and mobile. It also emphasizes the freedom that comes with being both a designer and a developer.

The two main slogans are “You’re still a creator when you’re off of the computer” and “Design by your own rules, create by your own code.”
